Sinigang Recipe

Sinigang is a traditional Filipino soup known for its sour flavor. It is typically made with pork, beef, chicken, or fish, along with a variety of vegetables, all simmered in a tamarind-based broth.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb of any meat of your choice, sliced
  • 1 onion, quartered
  • 2 tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 eggplant, sliced (optional)
  • 1 radish, sliced (optional)
  • 1 bunch kangkong (water spinach)
  • 1 packet tamarind soup base mix
  • 8 cups water
  • Fish Sauce
  • Pepper for Seasoning
  • Chili (optional)

Steps

  1. In a pot, bring water to a boil.
  2. Add Meat then boil until tender for about 15-20 minutes.
  3. Lower the heat, then add onion and tomatoes. Simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. Add the tamarind soup base mix and stir until dissolved.
  5. Add eggplant, radish, and chili, and simmer until vegetables are cooked for about 10 minutes.
  6. Season with fish sauce and pepper to taste.
  7. Add kangkong and steam for an additional 2 minutes.
  8. Adjust seasoning if necessary, then serve hot best with rice.
